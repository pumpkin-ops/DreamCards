# DreamCards Art Direction

## Creative North Star

A quiet dream archive appears on a warm tabletop after dusk. Players gather around images that feel discovered rather than manufactured. The room recedes; illuminated cards and half-formed stories remain.

## Core Qualities

- Dreamlike, suggestive, and emotionally open
- A physical tabletop gathering, not a digital dashboard
- Warm directional light with restrained cool accents
- Premium paper-card tactility
- Low text density
- Artwork as the dominant visual center

## Color and Light

- Base the environment on near-black charcoal, deep green, muted plum, and warm wood-neutral tones.
- Use amber or soft gold as the primary light source.
- Reserve cyan and violet for subtle magical accents, focus, and interaction feedback.
- Avoid a one-note blue/purple interface.
- Avoid bright gradients covering large surfaces.
- Use glow as reflected light around cards, not decorative blobs.

## Cards

- Keep artwork large, uncropped where inspection matters, and visually unobstructed.
- Use restrained rounded corners, fine edge highlights, paper texture, and layered shadows.
- Hover should feel like lifting a physical card toward the player.
- Selected or dragged cards may receive a soft rim light; avoid thick neon outlines.
- Card backs should belong to the same dream-archive world.

## Table and Space

- The table is spatial context, not the hero.
- Keep fixed player positions so the room has a stable mental map.
- Use darkness and falloff around the perimeter to frame the artwork.
- Avoid card-shaped panels for generic UI sections.
- Do not stack decorative cards inside cards.

## Typography

- Use concise Chinese interface text.
- Treat clues as poetic lines with generous space and lighter weight.
- Keep operational labels small and secondary.
- Avoid oversized dashboard metrics, dense helper copy, and technical AI explanations.

## Motion

- Favor slow, short, physically motivated movement: lift, settle, slide, reveal.
- Keep hover feedback immediate and subtle.
- Use opacity and depth changes for phase transitions.
- Avoid constant ambient movement that competes with image interpretation.

## Quality Bar

The visual result should sit between a premium digital board game and a restrained art exhibition. It should never resemble an admin panel, generic SaaS dashboard, or mobile web form.
