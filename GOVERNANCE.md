# Governance

DreamCards currently uses a maintainer-led governance model.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers:

- define release scope and repository policy;
- review security, moderation, and hidden-information changes;
- keep CI and documentation usable;
- label and triage issues;
- explain rejected design changes with system-level reasoning;
- protect contributor and player data.

## Decision process

Routine fixes may be merged after review and passing CI.

Changes to game rules, moderation policy, data ownership, provider strategy, or public APIs require:

1. a written issue or design note;
2. alternatives and risks;
3. tests or a validation plan;
4. maintainer approval.

When consensus is not available, the lead maintainer makes the final decision and documents the rationale.

## Becoming a maintainer

Regular contributors may be invited after demonstrating:

- sustained, reviewable contributions;
- reliable issue triage or documentation work;
- sound judgment around safety and information boundaries;
- respectful collaboration;
- ability to maintain areas they change.

## Project continuity

If the lead maintainer becomes unavailable, active maintainers may nominate a successor. Repository ownership changes should preserve the Apache-2.0 license, contributor attribution, security contacts, and moderation history.
